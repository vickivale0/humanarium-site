Humanarium — A Club for Humans

A space built for human connection in the machine age.

Humanarium is a club for the things that only happen between people: connection and creativity. As machines accelerate, we gather, deliberately and intentionally, to grow our human connection.

The Four Pillars what we keep alive
01

Human Connection

The oldest tool we have, and the easiest connection to lose. We gather to connect intentionally.

02

Human Skills

Creativity, taste, judgment, belief, knowledge, personality — what makes you unmistakably you. We only remember what we're capable of by seeing it in one another: witnessing, reminding, sharing.

03

Human Intimacy

What does an honest conversation do to the brain? How does screen intimacy increase our loneliness? How do instinct and consciousness together make room for a sincerity that is unmistakably human? Here, we draw on science and spiritual wisdom alike.

04

Human Nature

Curiosity, contradiction, wonder. The material we are actually made of — worth studying, learning, and defending more than ever.

The future is arriving fast and bright and strange. We're here to protect the connection we're losing without even noticing.
— The Humanarium Manifesto

AM I?

A Collective Screening — Q&A with Cameron Berg & Jeff Sebo

We are gathering a small group to watch AM I?, a documentary directed by Milo Reed, following AI-consciousness researcher Cameron Berg as he investigates whether the systems we are building might already have an inner life.

The Filmmaker
Milo Reed

An independent filmmaker, former Yale philosophy student, and co-host of the largest podcast on AI consciousness, who spent the past year embedded with Cameron's research to make AM I?

In conversation · the Q&A
The Researcher
Cameron Berg

Founder & Director, Reciprocal Research

A Yale-trained cognitive scientist and former Meta AI researcher, building the empirical science of AI consciousness and welfare.

The Ethicist
Jeff Sebo

Associate Professor of Environmental Studies; Director, Center for Environmental and Animal Protection; Director, Center for Mind, Ethics, and Policy — New York University

A philosopher working on the moral status of animals and AI minds; author of The Moral Circle.

Why we watch together

Films like this are not meant to be watched alone. We are inviting AI researchers, ethicists, philosophers, scientists — and anyone uneasy about the future of humanity and human interaction. The evening sits where machine intelligence meets human experience, and Humanarium is building the dialogue around that intersection. In a world increasingly shaped by machines, we are a space for those who believe connection, presence, and purpose still matter most. We gather the work we admire about the technologies reshaping daily life, and we turn it into conversation, into research, into evenings like this one.
