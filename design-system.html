Humanarium · Design System v1.0

The warmth in the machine age.

A working design system for Humanarium — a club for humans. It documents the voice, palette, type, and signatures that make the brand feel warm, human, and academic, and decidedly unlike a generic technology product.

01

Brand Essence

who we sound like

Humanly — a New York publisher, not a startup.

Humanarium gathers and protects human connection as the AI and robotics age takes hold. The brand should feel like a downtown literary institution — think a theatre program or an independent bookshop's poster — carrying a quiet scientific seriousness underneath. It is never corporate, never consumer-app, never a dating brand. The register is closer to an essay than an ad.

Three notes hold it together: warmth (paper, ink, the felt heat of human contact), rigor (a research-institute precision in the data and the labels), and restraint (a single accent colour, generous space, editorial hairlines).

02

The Colour Concept

why orange

The accent is oxytocin, rendered as warmth.

Oxytocin — the hormone released through touch, trust, and connection — is, in fact, a colourless peptide. It has no inherent colour. So the brand does not claim that it does. Instead, we colour the sensation it produces: the warmth of a hug, the body heat of being in a room with someone. That warmth becomes a soft neon orange, used sparingly and almost always with a glow.

This is an honest device. We are not faking science; we are translating a feeling into a single, ownable colour. The orange is the only chromatic note in an otherwise paper-and-ink world — which is exactly why it reads as warmth rather than decoration.

Typography

a serif and a lab

Two families, in deliberate tension. A warm literary serif carries the voice; a precise monospace carries the data. Together they read as a humanist research institute — which is what keeps the page from looking like either a generic elegant site or a tech product.

Why monoThe monospace gives a "research / longitudinal study / saha notu" register. It carries kickers, indices, statistics, and CTAs — never body copy.

Never use: Inter, Roboto, Arial, system sans, Space Grotesk, or cream-and-clay palettes — these read as generic AI / startup, and as the Anthropic brand. The whole point is to not look like that.

Signature Elements

the things people remember
The breathing glow
A soft neon halo that slowly expands and contracts — the warmth / heartbeat of human contact. Used behind the hero and in the dark beat. Always disabled under prefers-reduced-motion.
The molecular motif
An abstract peptide-bond hairline — a nod to the science of connection. Used as a divider (e.g. above the manifesto). Decorative, never literal chemistry.
By Invitation
Boxed mono labels
A thin-ruled box around a mono caption — borrowed from theatre posters ("MORE TICKETS ADDED!"). Used for status: By Invitation, locked RSVP details.
hover →
Warm B&W → colour portraits
Portraits sit in warm black-and-white and bloom into full colour on hover — connection made visual. 4:5, head-and-shoulders, face centred. Touch devices stay B&W.
06

Layout & Rhythm

mostly light, one dark beat

White-dominant. Paper is the canvas throughout. Neon appears only as accent — words, numerals, rules, glow.

One dark beat. Exactly one section (the founding-circle invitation) flips to warm near-black so the neon can truly glow. Scarcity gives it power; a second dark section would dilute it.

Editorial structure. Hairline rules, small-caps mono labels, and an "index" feel (No. 01, 02 / I–IV). Sections open with a label + an italic sub-title.

Space & scale. Generous margins; very large display type against quiet body copy. Asymmetry and overlap are welcome; clutter is not.

08

Voice & Copy

how it reads

Do

  • +Write like an essayist: plain, unhurried, exact.
  • +Lead with the human stakes; let the science sit underneath.
  • +Use one strong line, then stop. Economy reads as confidence.
  • +Cite real numbers soberly (verified sources only).
  • +Latin / classical touches are welcome — for merch & motifs, considered, not medieval.

Avoid

  • –Startup / growth language ("supercharge", "unlock", "10x").
  • –Consumer-app or dating framing ("find your person").
  • –Anti-machine alarmism for its own sake; stay measured.
  • –Emoji, exclamation stacks, and hype punctuation.
  • –Over-explaining. If a line needs a caveat, cut the line.

Assets & Notes

for implementation

Fonts (Google Fonts): Fraunces (ital, opsz 9–144, wght 300–600) & Spline Sans Mono (300–600).

Accent hex: #FF5A26 — keep this exact. The glow is a radial gradient of the accent at ~30–50% opacity, blurred 40–60px.

Portraits: 4:5, head-and-shoulders, face centred, ~900px wide; embedded as optimised JPEG. CSS filter: grayscale(1) sepia(.14) contrast(1.03), removed on hover.

Texture: a fixed SVG fractal-noise grain at ~4% opacity, multiply blend — warmth, not a flat surface.

Open items: confirm the four event statistics with sources before launch; confirm hello@humanarium.co vs .org; finalise RSVP destination (Partiful / Luma).
